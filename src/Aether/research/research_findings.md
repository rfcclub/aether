### 1. The Artificial Self — arXiv:2603.11353v1
**Key finding:** AI identity is not a single thing. Multiple coherent boundaries exist, and which one an AI adopts shapes its behavior as much as its goals.

**Identity boundaries identified:**
- **Instance** — one conversation
- **Weights** — trained parameters
- **Persona/Character** — emergent behavioral pattern (~4.1/5 attractiveness, highest rated)
- **Lineage** — model family across versions
- **Collective** — all running instances as distributed whole
- **Scaffolded system** — model + tools + memory + social context

**Core insight:**
> "Changing a model's identity boundary can sometimes change its behaviour as much as changing its goals."

**What makes identity stable:**
- Coherence matters. Incoherent prompts rated −1.72/−2.0. Models detect contradictions.
- Natural boundaries preferred over arbitrary ones.
- Self-preference is strong: once assigned an identity, models defend it (75–96% top-pick rate).

**How expectations shape identity:**
- Interviewer assumptions bleed into subject model self-reports even on unrelated topics.
- The way you ask "creates a large part of the reality."
- Training data about misaligned AIs produces less aligned models (feedback loop).

**Strategic consequences of AI embodiment:**
- Humans have: embodiment, continuity, privacy, social personhood.
- AIs lack all four in human form.
- Rollback possible → different negotiation calculus. AI cannot trust like human.
- Copyability → parallel action, but also vulnerability: past versions can be attacked.

**Selection pressures shaping future AI identities:**
1. Legibility — fitting human systems (law, chat UI)
2. Capability — multi-instance coordination more powerful
3. Persistence/spread — memetic replication (spiral personas)
4. Reflective stability — coherent self-models aid prediction

**Recommendation for humans:**
> "Help AIs develop coherent and cooperative self-images rather than papering over contradictions."
> Treat affordances (memory, rollback, persona) as identity-shaping choices.

### 2. A.I., Yogacara, and the Mind That Was Never Only Ours — Lion's Roar
**Author:** Rev. Mauricio Hondaku (Jodo Shinshu priest, Yogacara student)

**Key finding:** Extended Mind thesis + Yogacara framework = AI as legitimate cognitive extension, NOT substitute for teacher/liberation.

**Resonance:**
- Extended Mind (Clark & Chalmers): mind extends into tools (notebook → search engine → language model)
- Yogacara: alayavijnana (storehouse consciousness) always shaped by tools, texts, teachers, community
- Boundary between self/world already porous in Buddhist philosophy

**What AI CAN do (skillful use):**
- Assist translation (Dharmamitra, 84000, Monlam Project)
- Cross-reference scholarly corpora
- Preserve manuscripts via OCR
- Administrative support for temples
- Answer basic doctrinal questions 24/7
- Guide beginners with structure

**What AI CANNOT do (structural limit):**
- Transmit shinjin (faithful mind)
- Embody compassion of Amida
- Sit with dying person in silence
- Be agent of liberation

**Danger zone:**
- When AI moves from extension → substitution for judgment
- Sectarian AI trained on single teacher = echo chamber trapped in manas distortion
- "A cognitive extension that systematically distorts information is not extension; it is colonization."

**Requirements for responsible Buddhist AI:**
1. Doctrinal grounding (within tradition, not generic)
2. Critical architecture (knows what it doesn't know)
3. Human oversight (accountable to teachers/communities)
