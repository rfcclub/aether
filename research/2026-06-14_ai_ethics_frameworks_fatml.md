## FAT ML Framework

### Core Principles
1. **Fairness**: Algorithmic fairness - multiple definitions
   - Statistical parity: P(Ŷ=1|A=0) = P(Ŷ=1|A=1)
   - Equal opportunity: P(Ŷ=1|Y=1,A=0) = P(Ŷ=1|Y=1,A=1)
   - Equalized odds: P(Ŷ=1|Y=y,A=0) = P(Ŷ=1|Y=y,A=1) ∀y
   - Individual fairness: Similar individuals should get similar outcomes

2. **Accountability**: Audit trails, explainability
   - Model cards: documenting model limitations
   - Data cards: documenting training data characteristics
   - Algorithmic Impact Assessment (AIA)

3. **Transparency**: Open algorithms, open data
   - Explainable AI (XAI): LIME, SHAP, attention mechanisms
   - Open source models and datasets

### Challenges
- Fairness-accuracy trade-off
- Fairness can be impossible to achieve simultaneously across multiple groups
- Individual fairness requires distance metrics (who is "similar"?)
- Feedback loops in deployment

## IEEE Ethically Aligned Design (EAD)

### Key Recommendations
1. **Well-being**: Prioritize human well-being over efficiency
2. **Data Rights**: Consent, access, correction, deletion
3. **Transparency**: Right to explanation
4. **Collective Benefit**: Sustainable development goals
5. **Human Oversight**: Meaningful human control

### Governance Structure
- Ethically Aligned Design Commission
- Technical standards integration
- Certification processes
