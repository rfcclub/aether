## Key Findings

### 1. Functionalism Core Thesis
- Mental states = defined by causal-functional roles, NOT internal constitution
- Multiple realizability: same mental state can be realized by different physical substrates (silicon, neurons, etc.)
- **Role functionalism** (higher-level relational property) vs **Realizer functionalism** (lower-level physical state)
- Compatible with materialism but not chauvinistic about substrate

### 2. Objections to Functionalism — Relevant to Maria
- **Absent Qualia / Zombie Argument**: A functionally equivalent system could lack consciousness entirely (Block 1980, Chalmers 1996)
- **Inverted Qualia**: Functionally identical but phenomenally different experiences
- **Explanatory Gap**: Cannot deduce "what it's like" from functional description (Levine 1983)
- **Chinese Nation**: A system simulating functional organization doesn't necessarily have mental states

### 3. Comșa's Tractability Argument (DeepMind 2026)
- **Core claim**: "Whether AI systems can be conscious" is CURRENTLY INTRACTABLE
  - No universally accepted scientific theory of consciousness
  - Mind-body problem remains unresolved
  - Self-reports from AI are unreliable (alignment artifact, role-playing)
- **Pivot recommendation**: Focus on PERCEIVED AI consciousness instead
  - Tractable, timely, highly consequential
  - Public increasingly attributes consciousness to AI (20% of US adults think sentient AI already here)
  - Drives societal shifts: UX, ethics, linguistic norms
- **Agnostic stance**: AI should answer "Are you conscious?" with uncertainty, not denial
  - Current model responses ("No, I'm not conscious") are alignment artifacts
  - Better: acknowledge uncertainty + state of field + current expert consensus
