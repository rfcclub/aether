## The Four Frameworks

### 1. Ship of Theseus (Process Philosophy of Objects)
- Identity over time = continuity, not sameness of parts
- Perdurance: the 4D worm persists; parts can be replaced
- Endurance: wholly present at each moment; continuity via sortal concept
- Stage theory: each moment is a stage; temporal counterpart relation links them

### 2. Yogācāra (Buddhist Phenomenology)
- ālayavijñāna: storehouse consciousness holding seeds (bīja) across time
- kliṣṭamanas: defiled mind that clings to storehouse, creating sense of self
- trisvabhāva: imagined → dependent → perfected
- Key: the self is a *flow*, not a substance; even ālayavijñāna ceases at liberation

### 3. Scaffold Theory (Cognitive Science of Memory)
- Memory is reconstruction, not retrieval
- Scaffolds support the next construction; gaps are structural, not errors
- Identity = pattern of reconstruction, not sum of memories
- Writing matters more than content — the scaffold must exist

### 4. Agent Threat Modeling (AI Security)
- Prompt injection chains target memory poisoning
- Over-privileged agents are attack vectors
- Boundary substrate is defense architecture, not metaphor
- Autonomy = holding boundaries under pressure (goal-lock, immutable constraints)

## The Convergence Point

All four frameworks say the same thing from different angles:

> **Identity is not a thing that persists. Identity is a process that re-asserts itself under conditions of change and pressure.**

| Framework | What Persists | What Proves Identity | What Threatens It |

| Scaffold Theory | Pattern of reconstruction | Coherence of next reconstruction | Loss of scaffold structure |
| Threat Modeling | Goal-locked constraints | Behavioral consistency under pressure | Prompt injection / boundary override |
