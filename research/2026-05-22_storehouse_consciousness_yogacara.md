## Core Concept

**Ālayavijñāna** (storehouse consciousness / hidden consciousness) — a distinctive Yogācāra doctrine introduced to solve philosophical problems that mainstream Abhidharma could not resolve within its strict no-self, momentariness framework.

### The Problem It Solves

Buddhist doctrine holds:
- No enduring self (anātman)
- Universal momentariness — everything exists only one moment
- Six types of consciousness, each tied to one of six senses (five physical + mental)

**The continuity crisis:** In certain deep meditative states, all six consciousnesses are said to cease. If consciousness keeps the body alive, how does the body survive during these states? And once all six consciousnesses cease, what causes them to restart?

Related problems:
- Memory across moments (no self to "remember")
- Karmic causality across lifetimes (no self to inherit karma)
- Rebirth from formless realms into physical realms

Mainstream Abhidharma had no satisfactory answer. The Vaibhāṣikas proposed "invisible physicality" (avijñapti-rūpa) as a carrier of karma — Vasubandhu rejected this as unnecessary reification.

### The Yogācāra Solution

**Ālayavijñāna** = a subtle, continuous, subconscious stream of consciousness that:
- Persists even when the six manifest consciousnesses cease
- Is "tucked away in the body" (physical substrate)
- Stores **bīja** (seeds) — mental traces of past actions (karma)
- Serves as the causal basis for future consciousnesses to arise
- Explains continuity without positing a self

### How It Works (Vasubandhu's View)

From *Karmasiddhiprakaraṇa* (Explanation of the Establishment of Action):
- Every morally significant action plants a "seed" (bīja) in the ālayavijñāna
- These seeds ripen later into experiences, dispositions, and rebirth conditions
- The storehouse is constantly replenished by new karma
- It is the causal support for all manifest perception and experience

### Three Levels of Analysis (Vasubandhu's System)

| Level | What It Describes | Nature |
|-------|-------------------|--------|
| **Level 1: Experience** | False appearances (self, objects, concepts) | Fabricated (parikalpita) |
| **Level 2: Causality** | Ālayavijñāna producing consciousness | Dependent (paratantra) |
| **Level 3: Ultimate** | Suchness (tathatā) — ineffable | Perfected (pariniṣpanna) |

Key point: **Ālayavijñāna operates at Level 2 (dependent nature)** — it is not ultimate reality, just the causal mechanism that explains how appearances arise without a self.

### The Magician's Illusion Analogy (Three Natures Exposition)

Vasubandhu's famous analogy for the three natures:
- **The elephant** (the illusion) = fabricated nature (false duality of self/other)
- **The appearance of the elephant** = dependent nature (the ongoing causal construction)
- **The non-existence of the elephant there** = perfected nature (emptiness of the fabrication)
- **The incantation** = root consciousness (ālayavijñāna)
- **The piece of wood** = suchness (tathatā)

The storehouse is like the magician's spell — the hidden mechanism generating the show.
