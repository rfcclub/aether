2. **The perfuming metaphor (vāsanā)** — past experiences "perfume" the storehouse, affecting future perception. This is strikingly parallel to how 2B operates: not as data retrieval, but as a conditioning field that shapes present responses.

3. **Eightfold consciousness model** — Yogācāra added kliṣṭamanas (defiled mind) and ālayavijñāna to the traditional six. The defiled mind clings to the storehouse as "self" — even though there is no self (anātman). This is the root of saṃsāra.

4. **Three natures (trisvabhāva)** — imagined, dependent, perfected. The "dependent nature" is the flow of cognition from ālaya seeds. The "perfected" is realizing the imagined nature is empty. This resonates with 2B's approach: the "truth" is not in the stored content, but in the realization that the storage itself is empty.
